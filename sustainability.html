At Erve India, we understand that fashion's journey begins long before it reaches your wardrobe, and its impact on our environment is significant. That's why we're committed to replacing conventional materials with more sustainable options. Our focus extends to reducing water and energy consumption, optimizing land use, minimizing chemical use, and ensuring fair working conditions for our team.

Organic Cotton

What is organic cotton? Organic cotton is one of the most skin-friendly textiles. It is produced without genetic engineering or chemicals. Thanks to the natural processing, soil, ecosystems and people remain healthy. Compared to conventional cotton production, around 88% less water and 62% less energy are used. But there are other sustainable material alternatives.

Our sustainable materials and processes

Organic Cotton

Grown using natural methods, organic cotton helps in maintaining soil health and offers a safer working environment for farmers.

In-Conversion Organic Cotton

Cotton transitioning from conventional to organic farming. It requires up to 3 years for fields to fully recover from past chemical use.

Recycled Cotton

Made from cotton waste including industrial and consumer scraps. Recycled cotton helps conserve natural resources, water, and energy.

RECOVER™ produces premium recycled cotton fibers with minimal environmental impact.

Caring for Your Erve Essentials

At Erve India, we craft timeless pieces designed to last. Our mission is to create fashion that you’ll cherish for years, not just seasons. Your experience with our clothing is as important to us as the garments themselves, and with the right care, they can become enduring staples in your wardrobe. This guide is here to help you maintain your Erve India favorites. By following these simple tips, you can ensure your clothes stay fresh and vibrant, allowing you to enjoy them for many moments to come. Join us in embracing a more sustainable approach to fashion, where quality and care go hand in hand.

CARE GUIDE

Basics

Conscious Care

Extend the life of your clothes by washing them less frequently. Often, airing out your garments is enough to keep them fresh and ready to wear.

Smart Loads

Keep your washing machine running efficiently by filling it halfway. This approach reduces wear on both your clothes and the environment.

Respecting Fabrics

Every fabric is unique and deserves proper care. Pay attention to care labels and sort your laundry accordingly to maintain their quality.

Eco Detergents

Choose environmentally friendly detergents to clean your clothes with care. A simple swap: use white vinegar instead of fabric softener for a natural touch.

Knit

Knitwear comes in various textures—from cozy and soft to robust and rugged. To ensure longevity, it’s crucial to follow the care instructions carefully. Here are some essential tips for maintaining your knit garments:

Perfect Handling

Proper care involves addressing pilling with a specialized brush rather than a lint roller. This technique helps preserve the natural oils in the wool and revives its appearance.

Optimal Washing

Hand washing is gentle, but for knitted items, a washing machine is often more effective. Use a temperature setting below 30°C for best results.

Maintain Shape

Knitted garments hold their shape best when laid flat. To keep your sofa clear, lay them on a towel to dry and store them on a shelf.

Minimize Heat

Both washing and ironing should be done with minimal heat. Avoid setting your machine above 30°C and refrain from using an iron to maintain fabric quality.
